üîí Confidentiality

What is Confidentiality?

Confidentiality ensures that sensitive information is only accessible to authorized individuals. Think of it as keeping secrets safe from prying eyes.

Real-World Examples:

  • Password Protection: Your login credentials should only be known by you
  • Medical Records: Only authorized healthcare providers can access patient information
  • Financial Data: Bank account details are encrypted and protected
  • Company Secrets: Trade secrets and confidential business information

How We Maintain Confidentiality:

Encryption

Converting data into coded format

Access Controls

User permissions and authentication

Data Classification

Labeling information by sensitivity level

What is Integrity?

Integrity ensures that data remains accurate, complete, and unchanged during storage or transmission. It's about maintaining the trustworthiness of information.

Real-World Examples:

  • Email Authentication: Ensuring emails haven't been tampered with during delivery
  • Software Updates: Verifying that downloaded software hasn't been modified by malicious actors
  • Financial Transactions: Making sure transaction amounts aren't altered
  • Database Records: Preventing unauthorized changes to stored information

How We Maintain Integrity:

Digital Signatures

Cryptographic proof of authenticity

Checksums/Hashes

Mathematical verification of data

Version Control

Tracking changes and maintaining history

‚ö° Availability

What is Availability?

Availability ensures that systems, applications, and data are accessible and operational when authorized users need them. It's about keeping things running smoothly.

Real-World Examples:

  • Website Uptime: Ensuring websites are accessible 24/7
  • Emergency Services: 911 systems must always be available
  • Banking Systems: ATMs and online banking working when customers need them
  • Email Services: Being able to send and receive emails reliably

How We Maintain Availability:

Redundancy

Backup systems and multiple servers

Load Balancing

Distributing traffic across multiple resources

Disaster Recovery

Plans to restore systems after failures

What is Non-Repudiation?

Non-repudiation prevents someone from denying that they performed a specific action or sent a particular message. It provides proof of origin and delivery.

Real-World Examples:

  • Digital Contracts: Electronic signatures that can't be denied later
  • Email Receipts: Proof that an email was sent and received
  • Financial Transactions: Bank records showing who authorized a payment
  • Legal Documents: Timestamped and digitally signed court filings

How We Achieve Non-Repudiation:

Digital Signatures

Cryptographic proof of identity

Audit Logs

Detailed records of all actions

Timestamps

Precise timing of events and actions
